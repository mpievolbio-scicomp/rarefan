RAyt and REpin Finder and ANalyzer (RAREFAN)

The RAREFAN webserver aims to identify and analyze RAYT transposases and their associated REPINs in bacterial species. The input to the server is a selection of closely related strains (<5% divergence). There are three example datasets from Neisseria, E. coli, and P. chlororaphis. Neisseria and E. coli both contain Group 2 RAYTs (mainly found in enterobacteria), whereas P. chlororaphis contains Group 3 RAYTs (mainly found in Pseudomonas putida, P. fluorescens and P. syringae), for more explanation see Bertels, Gallie and Rainey 2017 (link https://academic.oup.com/gbe/article/9/8/2110/4061298). The webserver provides an analysis of REPIN population size, how it relates to REPIN replication rate and the presence and absence of RAYTs across all submitted genomes.
